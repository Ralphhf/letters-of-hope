How to find someone, what you can send, and the mail rules that keep your letter from being turned away.

You can start right here on our Write to Prisoners page. To search more broadly, use the free Federal Bureau of Prisons Inmate Locator for federal inmates, or your state's Department of Corrections (DOC) "offender search" for state facilities. You'll need the person's full name or ID number, and the tool will show their current facility and mailing address.

Keep it short, warm, and honest. Introduce yourself by first name, mention that you wanted to reach out, and share something ordinary — a book you're reading, the weather, a bit of encouragement or prayer. You don't need to discuss their charges or sentence; let them lead if they want to. Sincerity matters far more than length.

You must include the person's full committed name and their ID/inmate number, followed by the exact facility address. Without the ID number, mail is often returned. Always add your own return address — a P.O. box is perfectly fine if you'd rather not share your home address.

Rules vary by facility, but generally you can send plain handwritten or typed letters and a few standard photos. Commonly banned items include: cash, stamps, stickers, glitter, crayon or marker, laminated items, Polaroids, anything with a strong scent, and inappropriate images. When in doubt, stick to paper and blue or black ink. Check the specific facility's website before sending anything unusual.

Yes, letter-writing is a well-established ministry. Protect your privacy by using a P.O. box for your return address and sharing only what you're comfortable with. Never send money, gift cards, or account information, and be cautious of any request for financial help. Trust builds slowly — there's no need to rush.

Expect anywhere from a few days to several weeks. All incoming and outgoing mail is screened by the facility, which adds time, and postage or paper isn't always easy for someone inside to come by. Patience and consistency are the real gift — keep writing even before you hear back.
