How it works

Three steps to your first letter

You don't need the right words — you just need to show up.

1

Choose someone

Browse people on our Write to Prisoners page. Each listing includes their name, facility, and mailing address — everything you need to reach them.

2

Write your letter

Introduce yourself. Share an ordinary day, a bit of encouragement, a prayer. Kindness matters far more than eloquence.

3

Mail it safely

Follow each facility's simple mail rules — we walk you through them in our FAQ — then drop it in the mail. That's it.

Why it matters

Breaking the silence of a cell

Isolation is its own kind of sentence. Many incarcerated people — a great number of them Catholic, though not all — spend years without contact from the outside world. A letter says something a system never can: you still matter.

PHOTO
PLACEHOLDER
On the stigma

Writing isn't condoning

There's an old stigma around writing to prisoners. We're here to gently set it aside. Correspondence isn't excusing the past — it's affirming a future, and the God-given dignity of every person in it.
