The work of mercy

How a letter begins

I.

Choose someone

Browse the men and women on our Write to Prisoners page. Each listing carries a name, a facility, and a mailing address — everything you need to reach them.

II.

Write with kindness

Introduce yourself. Share an ordinary day, a word of encouragement, a prayer. Sincerity matters far more than eloquence.

III.

Send it with care

Follow the facility's simple mail rules — our FAQ walks you through them — and drop it in the mail. Mercy travels for the price of a stamp.

Why we write

Breaking the silence of a cell

Isolation is its own kind of sentence. Many incarcerated people — a great number of them Catholic, though not all — spend years without contact from the outside world. A letter says something a system never can: you still matter.

Writing a prisoner isn't condoning a crime. It's affirming a future, and the God-given dignity of every person in it.
